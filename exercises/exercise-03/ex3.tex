\aufgabe{Optimality in d dimensions}{

Let $\mathbf{X}$ be a $d$-dimensional random vector and let $\mathbf{Y}$ be a one-dimensional random vector with $\var(\mathbf{X}) = \mathbf{\Sigma}_{\mathbf{X}}$ and $\cov(\mathbf{X}, \mathbf{Y}) = \mathbf{\Sigma}_{\mathbf{X}, \mathbf{Y}} \in \R^{d \times 1}.$ Further, let $f:\R^d \rightarrow \R, \mathbf{w} \mapsto \var(\mathbf{w}^\top \mathbf{X} - \mathbf{Y})$, where $\mathbf{w} \in \R^{d}$ is non-random.

\begin{enumerate}
	\item Show that $f$ is convex.
	\item Compute $\nabla f$ and $\nabla^2 f$
	\item Under which condition exists a unique minimizer $\mathbf{w}^*$ of $f$. Is this a global minimum? (if it exists)

\end{enumerate}
